Walk Cycle & Hair Simulation
7/27/23
Autodesk Maya, XGen
I modeled, shaded, rigged, animated, and then added realistic fur to a character in Autodesk Maya.

Process
❮ ❯
(1/5) CHARACTER DESIGN:

 For this assignment, we were first told to design a bipedal character that we would later animate walking. Mine is based off an anteater! Specifically, the Tamandua Tetradactyla species. They stand up when they feel threatened.
(2/5) MODELING & SHADING

  • Refining my model, adding more and more geometry.
  • Using the Maya brush tool and Photoshop to make the fur texture.
(3/5) RIGGING
(4/5) ANIMATION First attempt at a walk cycle
  • Upper body is very stiff. More swaying of hips and torso would help.
  • The character is top heavy, so emphasizing this would add a lot to the walk.
(4.5/5) ANIMATION Revised walk cycle
  • More secondary motion in the upper torso.
  • More relaxed posture.
  • Also brightened up the image texture of the character.
(5/5) FUR DYNAMICS
  • Learned how to simulate fur in XGen.
  • Deciding between smooth vs. ruffled fur and amount of noise, levels of shine, and hair length.
